Bhuwa Naach: The War Dance from Far West

Bhuwa Naach is a culturally significant war dance performed in the hilly regions of Sudurpaschim Province, especially in Doti. This powerful dance embodies martial spirit and religious dedication.

Male participants, dressed in white, carry wooden or metal swords and shields, performing synchronized movements that mimic ancient battle formations. Traditional instruments like madals accompany the dance, intensifying its war-like atmosphere.

The dance is deeply rooted in history, dating back to the Baise-Chaubise Rajya era. Communities such as Tikha, Bagthanta, and Gholtada observe Bhuwa Naach during post-monsoon festivals, usually in temple premises like Kaladunga Bhagwati.

Bhuwa Naach is more than a performance—it is a spiritual ritual to honor deities like Bhagwati, invoke protection, and celebrate collective heritage. It also serves as a living tradition that connects generations through valor, discipline, and devotion.

Today, Bhuwa Naach continues to inspire community pride and reinforces the rich identity of Sudurpaschim’s cultural landscape.

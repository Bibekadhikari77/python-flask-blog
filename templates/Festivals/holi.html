Holi Festival in Sudurpaschim

Holi, the vibrant festival of colors, is joyfully celebrated throughout Sudurpaschim Province, bringing together people of all ages and communities. It marks the arrival of spring and symbolizes the victory of good over evil.

People gather in towns, homes, and streets to throw colorful powders called abir, sing folk songs, and dance to traditional and modern beats. Elders exchange greetings and sweets like gujiya and malpua.

Community events, music, and cultural shows create a strong sense of unity and joy. Holi in Sudurpaschim is more than just color—it’s about bonding, laughter, and tradition.

Bonfires known as Holika Dahan are lit on the eve of Holi, symbolizing the story of Prahlad and Holika, where good triumphs over evil. People gather, sing, and offer prayers around the fire.

Holi also becomes a time for healing—people forgive, reconnect, and strengthen friendships. With growing awareness, Sudurpaschim celebrates Holi using herbal, eco-friendly colors to protect both health and nature.

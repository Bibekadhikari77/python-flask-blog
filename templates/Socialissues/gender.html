Gender Equality in Sudurpaschim Province

In Sudurpaschim Province, gender inequality has historically been deep-rooted in traditional customs and patriarchal norms. Women were often confined to household responsibilities, deprived of education, and excluded from economic participation and leadership roles.

Local women’s cooperatives and microfinance groups have enabled thousands of women to start small businesses, become financially independent, and contribute to household income. Women-led enterprises are gaining ground in agriculture, handicrafts, and services.

Today, women in Sudurpaschim are breaking traditional barriers. From becoming school teachers, nurses, and government officers to contesting local elections and leading NGOs, they are shaping the province's progress.

Despite challenges like gender-based violence and limited access to reproductive health, ongoing efforts from government and civil society continue to push for equality, inclusion, and empowerment of all genders.
